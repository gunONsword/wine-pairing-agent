# Wine Pairing — Red Meat, Game & BBQ

The default for red meat is red wine, but *which* red depends heavily on cut, fat content, cooking method, sauce, and aging. This skill drills into each combination.

Default cross-reference: read `wine-pairing-fundamentals` first.

---

## 1. The general framework for red meat

Red meat is dominated by **protein + fat + Maillard reactions** (when seared or grilled). The wine needs:
- **Tannin** to bind to the fat and protein (softens in the mouth, releases fruit notes).
- **Body / structure** to match the dish's weight.
- **Acidity** to lift the richness and cleanse the palate.
- **Aromatics** that either mirror the dish (smoky red on grilled meat = concordance) or complement (fruity red on braised meat = contrast).

For game (venison, wild boar, hare): same logic but turned up — more intense aromas, more iron-y/gamey notes, demands a wine with more spice, leather, undergrowth notes.

## 2. Beef

### 2.1 Grilled steak / ribeye / sirloin / T-bone (à feu vif)

- **Profile sought**: Full-bodied red, high tannin, medium-high acidity, ripe black fruit, sometimes oak.
- **Concrete grapes/regions**:
  - **Cabernet Sauvignon** — Napa Valley, Bordeaux (Médoc, Pauillac, Saint-Estèphe), Coonawarra (Australia)
  - **Malbec** — Mendoza (Argentina), Cahors (France, "black wine")
  - **Syrah / Shiraz** — Northern Rhône (Hermitage, Côte-Rôtie), Barossa Valley
  - **Sangiovese / Brunello / Chianti Classico Riserva** — Tuscany (bistecca alla fiorentina canonical match)
  - **Nebbiolo** — Barolo, Barbaresco (Piedmont) — austere but right for well-marbled cuts
- **Abstract attributes**: tannin high, body full, alcohol 13-15 %, oak optional but common, fruit black (blackberry, cassis, plum), savoury notes (tobacco, cedar, graphite for Bordeaux; pepper for Syrah).

### 2.2 Beef tartare / carpaccio (raw)

- Profile: medium-bodied red with low tannin and high acidity, OR rich fruity white.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Beaujolais Cru** (Morgon, Fleurie) — juicy Gamay, fresh
  - **Pinot Noir** — Burgundy, Oregon, Sancerre rouge
  - **Light Chianti** (not Riserva)
- Some sommeliers go to a white: **Chablis** or aged **Chardonnay** to contrast the lean meat's iron notes.

### 2.3 Braised beef / Boeuf Bourguignon / brasato / pot-au-feu

- Profile: medium-full red with **soft tannins**, fresh acidity, and earthy/savoury aromas.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Burgundy Pinot Noir** (canonical for Boeuf Bourguignon — wine often cooked with the dish)
  - **Barolo / Barbera d'Asti** (for brasato al Barolo, Piedmont)
  - **Côtes du Rhône** — Grenache-Syrah blends
  - **Mencía** — Bierzo (Spain), savoury and earthy
- Avoid: very young, very tannic wines — the long-cooked dish wants soft fruit.

### 2.4 Beef stew / daube / carbonnade

- Profile: medium red, fruity, soft tannins.
- **Concrete**: Côtes du Rhône, Provence reds, Bandol, Beaujolais Crus, Tempranillo Crianza.

### 2.5 Beef in cream sauce (e.g. stroganoff) or with mushroom

- Profile: **white minéral** (contrast — acidity cuts cream) OR medium red with earthy/mushroom aromas.
- **Concrete**: Chablis, white Burgundy, Pinot Noir (Burgundy or Oregon), Savigny-lès-Beaune, Santenay rouge.

### 2.6 Joue de bœuf (beef cheek) / osso buco

- Profile: medium-full red with structure but soft.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Saint-Estèphe** (Bordeaux) for joue de bœuf bordelaise — the local pairing
  - **Barolo / Nebbiolo** for ossobuco (Piedmont)
  - **Brunello di Montalcino**

### 2.7 Bistecca alla fiorentina (Tuscan T-bone)

- Profile: full-bodied Tuscan red.
- **Concrete**: **Chianti Classico Riserva**, **Brunello di Montalcino**, **Vino Nobile di Montepulciano**, Super Tuscans (Cab-Sangiovese blends).

## 3. Lamb

Lamb has a distinct gamey/herbaceous flavour. Its richer fat content benefits from tannins.

### 3.1 Roasted lamb (gigot, rack of lamb, slow-roast shoulder)

- Profile: full-bodied red, mature tannins, herbal/savoury aromas (thyme, rosemary, garrigue).
- **Concrete**:
  - **Rioja Gran Reserva** (Spanish canonical — Tempranillo)
  - **Bordeaux Cabernet-Merlot blends** (Pauillac, Saint-Julien, Margaux)
  - **Châteauneuf-du-Pape** (Grenache-Syrah-Mourvèdre)
  - **Bandol** (Mourvèdre, Provence)
  - **Napa Cabernet Sauvignon**
  - **Côtes du Rhône Villages**

### 3.2 Lamb shanks with olives (Mediterranean)

- Profile: medium-full red with herb and olive aromas.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Beaujolais Cru** (Morgon, Moulin-à-Vent)
  - **Côtes de Provence rouge**
  - **Saint-Joseph** (Northern Rhône Syrah)
  - **Agiorgitiko** (Greece — Nemea)

### 3.3 Lamb with apricots / Moroccan tagine / Middle-Eastern lamb

- Profile: medium red with sweet ripe fruit, lower tannin, spice notes — OR off-dry white.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Saint-Joseph** (Syrah with subtle white pepper)
  - **Châteauneuf-du-Pape blanc** (rich white for tagine)
  - **Gewürztraminer** (off-dry, for tagine with prunes/raisins)
  - **Côtes du Rhône**

### 3.4 Slow-cooked rack of lamb (rare, herb-crusted)

- Profile: structured full red with refined tannins.
- **Concrete**: Napa Cabernet Sauvignon, Margaux, Saint-Julien, fine Chianti Classico Gran Selezione.

### 3.5 Chuletillas de cordero al sarmiento (Spanish vine-shoot grilled lamb chops)

- Concrete: **Rioja Gran Reserva** (canonical).

### 3.6 Lamb kefta / brochettes

- Concrete: Côtes du Rhône, Languedoc, structured rosé (Bandol), Tempranillo.

## 4. Game (gibier)

Game = strong, iron-y, often slightly sweet aromatic notes; needs structured wines with leather/spice/undergrowth character.

### 4.1 Venison / deer / chevreuil

- Profile: full-bodied red, high tannin, peppery + spice + animal notes.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Northern Rhône Syrah** (Hermitage, Cornas)
  - **Châteauneuf-du-Pape**
  - **Barolo / Barbaresco**
  - **Sagrantino di Montefalco** (very tannic, Umbria)
  - **Madiran** (Tannat, southwest France)
  - **Saint-Émilion** (older vintages)

### 4.2 Wild boar / cinghiale (e.g. Pici al ragù di cinghiale)

- Profile: full red, savoury, often Italian.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Vino Nobile di Montepulciano**
  - **Chianti Classico Riserva**
  - **Brunello di Montalcino**
  - **Sagrantino**
  - **Aglianico del Vulture** (Basilicata)

### 4.3 Sauté de lapin (rabbit / hare)

- Profile: medium red with fresh fruit and gentle tannin.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Bordeaux rouge** (especially Bordeaux Supérieur, e.g. Château de Lionne — Merlot-dominant)
  - **Pinot Noir** (Burgundy, Sancerre rouge)
  - **Beaujolais Cru**

### 4.4 Faisan / pintade / guinea fowl

- Profile: structured white OR light red (these are *feathered* game).
- **Concrete**:
  - **White Burgundy** (rich, oaked Chardonnay)
  - **Pinot Noir** (Burgundy)
  - **Pomerol** (Merlot-dominant)

### 4.5 Bécasse à la ficelle / woodcock

- Concrete: mature Burgundy (Vosne-Romanée, Pommard).

### 4.6 Duck (canard) — wild and domestic

#### 4.6.1 Roast duck breast (magret) — rare

- **Pinot Noir** (Burgundy, Oregon) — canonical
- **Saint-Joseph** (Syrah)
- **Madiran**
- **Cahors** (Malbec)

#### 4.6.2 Confit de canard (Sud-Ouest)

- **Madiran**, **Cahors**, **Bergerac**, **Côtes de Saint-Mont** — all SW France
- **Rioja Reserva**

#### 4.6.3 Duck with cherries / caramelised apples / orange

- **Burgundy Pinot Noir** (with cherries)
- **Saint-Joseph** (with caramelised apples)
- **Vouvray demi-sec** or **Coteaux du Layon** (with orange — contrast)

#### 4.6.4 Roast duckling with Merlot-chocolate sauce

- **Long Island Merlot**, **Right-bank Bordeaux** (Saint-Émilion, Pomerol)

## 5. BBQ / grilled red meat

BBQ adds **smoke + caramelisation + often a sweet-savoury sauce**. You need a red that can stand up to all three.

### 5.1 BBQ ribs (pork or beef) / sausages / bacon cheeseburger

- Profile: full-bodied red with ripe fruit, smoke, and high alcohol that survives the sauce.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Zinfandel** — Lodi, Sonoma (canonical for American BBQ — berry burst + smoky)
  - **Shiraz** — Barossa Valley
  - **Malbec** — Mendoza
  - **Primitivo** — Puglia
  - **Côtes du Rhône** / **Châteauneuf-du-Pape**

### 5.2 Hamburger / steak frites grilled

- Concrete: **Syrah from Northern Rhône** (Saint-Joseph), **Sonoma Zinfandel**, **Malbec**, **Chianti Classico**.

### 5.3 Pulled pork / brisket (American BBQ)

- Concrete: **Zinfandel**, **Petite Sirah**, **Shiraz**, **Malbec**.

### 5.4 Asado argentino (mixed grilled meats)

- Concrete: **Malbec** (Mendoza canonical).

## 6. Cured / charcuterie (raw or barely-cooked red meat)

### 6.1 Jamón ibérico / ibérico bellota / serrano

- **Manzanilla** or **Fino** Sherry (canonical Andalucian pairing)
- **Cava Brut Nature**
- **Tempranillo Crianza** (Rioja)
- **Champagne**

### 6.2 Prosciutto crudo (Parma, San Daniele)

- **Lambrusco** (Emilia-Romagna canonical — fizzy red)
- **Franciacorta**
- **Trebbiano d'Abruzzo**

### 6.3 Rillettes de canard / pâté de campagne

- **Pauillac** or **Saint-Émilion**
- **Pinot Noir** or **Saint-Joseph**
- **Demi-sec Vouvray** (contrast)

### 6.4 Salame / soppressata / coppa / culatello

- **Lambrusco**, **Sangiovese**, **Barbera d'Asti**, **Dolcetto**

### 6.5 Saucisson sec / boudin noir

- **Beaujolais**, **Côtes du Rhône**, light **Bordeaux**, **Saint-Pourçain**

### 6.6 Carpaccio of beef

- **Light Pinot Noir** (Sancerre rouge, Bourgogne)
- **Franciacorta** or **Crémant**
- **Verdicchio**

## 7. Sauces — common red-meat pairings

| Sauce | Wine direction |
|---|---|
| Au poivre (peppercorn) | Syrah / Shiraz (concordance) |
| Bordelaise (red wine + shallot + marrow) | The same Bordeaux that made the sauce |
| Béarnaise | Rich Chardonnay or medium red (Merlot) |
| Roquefort sauce | Sauternes (contrast — sweet+blue) or fine Pomerol |
| Mushroom / forestière | Pinot Noir, Nebbiolo, aged Rioja |
| Chocolate / mole | Zinfandel, Malbec, Tempranillo Reserva |
| Demi-glace (any meat) | Rich tannic reds: Brunello, Cab, Châteauneuf |
| Curry / spiced (lamb tagine) | Off-dry Riesling, Gewürztraminer, or robust southern red |

---

## 8. Cuts & cooking heat (general schema)

| Cut/Heat | Wine direction |
|---|---|
| Rare / saignant (lots of blood, iron) | High tannin Cabernet, Nebbiolo |
| Medium-rare | Mid-tannin Sangiovese, Tempranillo, Merlot |
| Well-done (less juice, more crust) | Slightly softer red, Malbec, Beaujolais |
| Slow-braised (long, soft) | Soft fruity red, mature Burgundy, Côtes du Rhône |
| Smoked | Smoky concordance: Rioja, Syrah, Zinfandel |
| Sous-vide (gentle) | Medium-bodied with finesse: Pinot Noir, Sangiovese |

## 9. Abstract attribute mapping (for unfamiliar dishes)

If you've identified the dish's profile but don't know what to pour, use this:

| Need | Wine attribute | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Cut fat | Acidity OR tannin | Sangiovese, Cab, Riesling |
| Match smoke | Smoky / mineral | Rioja, Syrah, Northern Rhône |
| Match herbs (rosemary, thyme) | Mediterranean garrigue notes | Bandol, Châteauneuf, Provence rouge |
| Match pepper / spice | Syrah / Shiraz | Cornas, Crozes-Hermitage, Barossa |
| Match earthiness (truffle, mushroom) | Earthy tertiary aromas | aged Pinot Noir, Nebbiolo, aged Rioja |
| Match sweet glaze | Wine with ripe fruit / hint of sweetness | Zinfandel, Amarone, off-dry Riesling |
| Match iron / blood notes | High tannin, structured | Barolo, Cabernet, Madiran |
| Add freshness against richness | High acidity | Sangiovese, Chianti, Beaujolais |

## 10. Common mistakes

- **Tannic red + delicate fish / vegetable + red meat**: when a steak comes with vegetable side, focus on the steak, not the side.
- **High-alcohol red + spicy lamb tagine**: alcohol amplifies the chilli. Lower alcohol, go to softer GSM blend or rosé.
- **Light red + heavy braise**: the wine disappears. Match weight.
- **Same wine for every course at a meat-heavy meal**: serve a lighter wine first, build up. Beaujolais → Chianti → Barolo, not the reverse.

---

## 11. Quick decision tree

```
Is it red meat?
├── Grilled & rare → tannic Cab/Nebbiolo/Malbec
├── Slow-cooked/braised → soft mature red (Burgundy, Bordeaux Supérieur)
├── Raw (tartare, carpaccio) → light fresh red OR Champagne
├── Game (venison, boar) → structured Syrah/Barolo/Sagrantino
├── Lamb → Rioja, Bordeaux, Châteauneuf, Bandol
├── Duck → Pinot Noir or Saint-Joseph
├── Cured (charcuterie) → Fino Sherry, Lambrusco, light reds
└── BBQ → Zinfandel, Shiraz, Malbec
```
