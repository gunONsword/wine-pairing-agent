# Wine Pairing — White Meat (Poultry, Pork, Veal, Rabbit)

White meat is leaner, more delicate, and more sauce-driven than red meat. The wine choice often depends more on the sauce or cooking method than on the protein itself. Default: medium-bodied white or light-to-medium red.

Read `wine-pairing-fundamentals` first.

---

## 1. The general framework for white meat

- Leaner protein → less tannin needed.
- More **delicate flavour** → wine must not overpower.
- **Sauce dominates the pairing more than the meat**.
- Cream sauces, mushrooms, citrus, herbs, spices each take the dish in a different direction.

Default starting points:
- **Chardonnay (oaked)** for richer poultry/veal with butter or cream
- **Pinot Noir** for almost any white meat with restraint
- **Riesling / Gewürztraminer** for pork
- **Sauvignon Blanc** when herbs and citrus dominate
- **Champagne / sparkling** when richness + acidity needed

## 2. Chicken

### 2.1 Roast chicken (simple, butter, thyme)

The classic Sunday dish. Surprisingly demanding because of the crispy fatty skin + lean breast + caramelised pan juice.

- **Profile sought**: full white with butter, brioche, toasted notes, balanced acidity. Or light fresh red with no aggressive tannin.
- **Concrete (white)**:
  - **White Burgundy / Chardonnay** — Chassagne-Montrachet, Mercurey blanc, Montagny 1er Cru, Mâcon-Villages, Saint-Véran, Pouilly-Fuissé. **Canonical pairing.** Buttery, brioche, hazelnut, mineral finish.
  - **Australian Chardonnay** (cooler regions — Margaret River, Yarra Valley)
  - **Viognier** (rich, peach, apricot)
  - **White Rioja Reserva**
- **Concrete (red)**:
  - **Burgundy Pinot Noir** — cherry, soft tannins
  - **Beaujolais Cru** (Morgon, Fleurie) — juicy Gamay
- **Concrete (Champagne — gastronomic match)**:
  - **Blanc de Noirs** — vinous, brioche notes echo the skin
  - **Brut vintage** with toasted notes
- **Attributes**: medium-full body, medium acidity, moderate oak (just enough — not buttery-bomb), brioche/butter/hazelnut aroma.

### 2.2 Roast chicken with lemon / herbs / garlic

- Shift to a tenser, more citric white.
- **Concrete**: **Saint-Véran**, **Sancerre**, **Albariño**, **Vermentino**, **Verdicchio**, **Soave Classico**.

### 2.3 Roast chicken with mushroom or cream sauce

- Richer dish needs more volume.
- **Concrete**: **oaked Chardonnay** (Meursault, Puligny-Montrachet), **Viognier**, **Pinot Noir** with earthy notes (Santenay, Volnay), **Savigny-lès-Beaune** red.

### 2.4 Fried chicken (Kentucky-style)

- **Champagne** (canonical sommelier match — bubbles + acidity cut the grease)
- **Cava Brut**
- **Riesling** (off-dry)
- **Sauvignon Blanc**

### 2.5 Chicken curry (Thai, Indian)

- Off-dry whites that balance heat.
- **Concrete**: **off-dry Riesling** (Mosel, Alsace), **Gewürztraminer**, **Vouvray demi-sec**, **Pinot Gris Alsace**.

### 2.6 Chicken sate (Indonesian/Malaysian)

- **Australian Chardonnay** with tropical fruit, or **off-dry Riesling**.

### 2.7 Chicken tostadas / tacos / fajitas (Mexican)

- **Vouvray** (Chenin), **Albariño**, **Sauvignon Blanc**, **light Mexican Tempranillo**, **Rosé**.

### 2.8 Chicken liver pâté

- **Non-vintage Brut Rosé Champagne** (canonical)
- **Pinot Noir** (Burgundy)
- **Vouvray demi-sec**
- **Vin Santo** (with Tuscan crostini tradition)

### 2.9 Chicken paella

- **Albariño**, **Verdejo**, **dry Rosé from Navarra or Provence**, **Cava**.

### 2.10 Chicken & mushroom paella

- **Albariño** (canonical Spanish — fits chicken + mushroom + saffron + rice).

### 2.11 Coq au vin

- The wine used to cook with: **Burgundy red Pinot Noir** (canonical). Same producer, same level.

### 2.12 Chicken in white wine (poulet à la moutarde, Marengo)

- **Mâconnais white** (Saint-Véran, Pouilly-Fuissé) or **Pinot Noir** (light Burgundy).

### 2.13 Chinese / Asian sticky chicken (sweet-soy glaze)

- **Off-dry Riesling**, **Gewürztraminer**, **Pinot Noir** (for soy umami).

## 3. Turkey / Capon / Pheasant / Guinea fowl

### 3.1 Roast turkey (Thanksgiving, Christmas)

- Wide-ranging meal — multiple sides — so a versatile wine wins.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Pinot Noir** (the canonical American choice)
  - **Beaujolais Cru** (light enough for stuffing, sauce, sides)
  - **Zinfandel** (if BBQ-ish flavours)
  - **Champagne** for the toast
  - **Côtes du Rhône** (versatile)
  - **Off-dry Riesling** if cranberry sauce is prominent (sweet contrast)

### 3.2 Capon (chapon)

- Richer than chicken, often served at Christmas.
- **Concrete**: **Mâcon-Lugny**, **Meursault**, **Champagne Blanc de Blancs**.

### 3.3 Pheasant (faisan)

- Game-like but lighter than venison.
- **Concrete**: **mature Burgundy Pinot Noir**, **Pomerol** (Merlot), **rich Chardonnay**, **Pinot Gris Alsace** Réserve.

### 3.4 Guinea fowl (pintade)

- Lighter than pheasant.
- **Concrete**: **Mâconnais white** (Pouilly-Fuissé), **Pinot Noir** (Beaune, Mercurey), **Pinot Gris**.

### 3.5 Pintade / faisan in white wine sauce

- **Rich oaked white**: Meursault, Puligny-Montrachet, Hermitage blanc, Châteauneuf-du-Pape blanc.

## 4. Duck (see also wine-pairing-meat-red for richer/braised preparations)

### 4.1 Magret de canard (rare-cooked breast)

- More red-meat-like. See wine-pairing-meat-red §4.6.
- Quick reference: **Burgundy Pinot Noir**, **Saint-Joseph**, **Madiran**, **Cahors**, **Bandol**.

### 4.2 Duck confit

- Sud-Ouest reds: **Madiran**, **Cahors**, **Bergerac**.

### 4.3 Duck rillettes / pâté

- **Pauillac**, **Saint-Émilion**, **Vouvray demi-sec** (contrast).

### 4.4 Roast duckling with fruit (apple, orange, cherry)

- **Mature Burgundy Pinot Noir** (cherry → cherry concordance)
- **Saint-Joseph** (caramelised apples)
- **Vouvray demi-sec** (orange — contrast)

### 4.5 Peking duck (Chinese, sweet plum sauce)

- **Pinot Noir** (Oregon, Burgundy)
- **Off-dry Riesling**
- **Gewürztraminer**

## 5. Pork

### 5.1 Roast pork loin / longe

- Pork loin is lean, mild — wines should not dominate.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Pinot Blanc** (Alsace) — clean, refreshing
  - **Riesling** (Alsace dry, Australia Clare/Eden Valley dry)
  - **Beaujolais Cru**
  - **Pinot Noir** (Burgundy, Oregon)
- With fruit sauce (apple, cider, plum):
  - **Off-dry Riesling**, **Vouvray demi-sec**, **rich Chardonnay**

### 5.2 Pork belly / panceta / lechón / crispy pork

- High fat, crispy skin → needs acidity or sweet contrast.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Off-dry Riesling** (canonical contrast — the most reliable pairing)
  - **Gewürztraminer**
  - **Pinot Noir** (Burgundy)
  - **Champagne / sparkling**

### 5.3 Pork chops (côte de porc) — grilled or pan-fried

- **Concrete**:
  - **Pinot Noir** (canonical for grilled with Seville orange / fruit notes)
  - **Beaujolais Cru**
  - **Côtes du Rhône**
  - **Riesling Spätlese trocken**

### 5.4 Pork in milk and rosemary (Italian — rôti de porc confit au lait et romarin)

- **Concrete**: **Merlot-Syrah blend** (Côtes du Rhône, Provence, IGP Méditerranée — see Cuvée Le Benjamin, Domaine Grand-Père Jules), **Côtes de Provence**, **Bandol rouge**.

### 5.5 Schweinshaxe / codillo / German pork knuckle

- High fat, big roast — needs **structure + freshness**.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Spätburgunder** (German Pinot Noir)
  - **Riesling Trocken** (Rheingau, Mosel) — for contrast
  - **Cabernet Sauvignon**, **Syrah**, **Tempranillo** (full reds, contrast against the fat)
  - **Beer** (traditional partner — not wine, but worth noting)

### 5.6 Pork sausages / saucisses (German Bratwurst, Italian salsiccia, French boudin blanc)

- **Concrete**: **Gewürztraminer** (with Alsace choucroute), **Riesling**, **Lambrusco**, **Sangiovese**, **Beaujolais**, **Spätburgunder**.

### 5.7 Charcuterie pork (jamón ibérico, prosciutto, salame) — see wine-pairing-meat-red §6.

### 5.8 Pulled pork / BBQ pork ribs — see wine-pairing-meat-red §5.

### 5.9 Tonkatsu / breaded pork cutlet / schnitzel

- **Concrete**:
  - **Grüner Veltliner** (Austrian canonical for schnitzel)
  - **Riesling Trocken**
  - **Pinot Blanc**
  - **Sauvignon Blanc**

### 5.10 Sauerbraten (German braised marinated beef/pork)

- **Spätburgunder** (canonical — Pinot Noir from Pfalz, Baden, Ahr)
- **Riesling Spätlese** (off-dry, contrast)

### 5.11 Cured pork — jamón serrano, ibérico

- See wine-pairing-meat-red §6.1.

### 5.12 Honey ham / sweet-glazed ham

- **Off-dry Riesling**, **Gewürztraminer**, **Vouvray demi-sec**, **rosé Champagne**.

## 6. Veal

Veal is lean, sweet, delicate — choose finesse over power.

### 6.1 Veal scaloppine / piccata

- **Concrete**:
  - **Soave Classico**
  - **Verdicchio dei Castelli di Jesi**
  - **Pinot Grigio** (Friuli, Alto Adige)
  - **light Chianti** (not Riserva)
  - **Pinot Noir**

### 6.2 Vitello tonnato

- Cold, with tuna-mayo sauce.
- **Concrete**: **Soave**, **Gavi** (Cortese, Piedmont), **Pinot Grigio**, **Franciacorta**.

### 6.3 Blanquette de veau (cream sauce)

- **Concrete**:
  - **Rich oaked Chardonnay** (Meursault, Mâcon-Lugny)
  - **Viognier** (Condrieu)
  - **Champagne Blanc de Blancs**

### 6.4 Veau Marengo / veau aux olives / veau en sauce tomate

- **Concrete**: **Côtes du Rhône**, **Provence rouge**, **Bandol**, **Sangiovese**.

### 6.5 Veal Marengo (light Bordeaux pairing)

- Light Bordeaux, Provence rouge, Cabernet Franc.

### 6.6 Veal cheek / joue de veau braisée

- **Concrete**: **Pomerol** (Merlot), **Saint-Émilion**, **Burgundy** (Pommard, Mercurey rouge).

### 6.7 Veal kidney / ris de veau

- **Concrete**: rich Chardonnay, mature **Pinot Noir** (Vosne-Romanée).

### 6.8 Wiener schnitzel

- **Grüner Veltliner** (Austrian canonical), **Pinot Blanc**.

### 6.9 Osso buco (with risotto alla milanese — Lombardy)

- **Concrete**: **Barbera d'Alba**, **Nebbiolo**, **Barolo**, **Rocca di Frassinello** (Sangioveto-Cab-Merlot Tuscan blend).

### 6.10 Veal with apricots / fruit (sweet-sour preparations)

- **Riesling Spätlese**, **Pinot Gris Alsace** Réserve.

### 6.11 Fondant de veau au basilic et citron confit (Provence-style)

- **Bandol rosé** / **Côtes de Provence rouge** / **Cuvée Le Cadet** (Marsanne) — Domaine Grand-Père Jules.

## 7. Rabbit (lapin)

Lean game-adjacent meat.

### 7.1 Sauté de lapin aux girolles (rabbit with mushrooms)

- **Bordeaux Supérieur** (Merlot-dominant — canonical match, e.g. Château de Lionne)
- **Pinot Noir**
- **Beaujolais Cru**

### 7.2 Rabbit in mustard sauce (lapin à la moutarde)

- **Chablis**, **Burgundy white** (Mercurey blanc), **Sancerre**, **Pinot Noir**.

### 7.3 Rabbit with prunes (lapin aux pruneaux)

- **Saumur-Champigny** (Cabernet Franc), **Chinon**, **Côtes du Rhône**.

### 7.4 Rabbit pâté / rillettes

- **Vouvray demi-sec**, **rosé Champagne**, **light Pinot Noir**.

## 8. Sauces — common white-meat pairings

| Sauce | Wine direction |
|---|---|
| Cream / butter / béchamel | Oaked Chardonnay (concordance) or crisp Champagne (contrast) |
| Mushroom / forestière | Pinot Noir or rich oaked Chardonnay |
| Lemon / citrus / herb | Sauvignon Blanc, Sancerre, Verdicchio |
| Mustard | Chablis or Pinot Noir |
| Curry (mild) | Off-dry Riesling, Pinot Gris Alsace |
| Curry (spicy) | Gewürztraminer, off-dry Riesling Spätlese |
| Soy / teriyaki | Pinot Noir or off-dry Riesling |
| Wine reduction (red wine sauce) | The same wine that made the sauce |
| Apple / fruit sauce | Off-dry Riesling, Vouvray demi-sec |
| Honey / sweet glaze | Off-dry Riesling, Gewürztraminer |
| Tomato | Chianti, Sangiovese, Tempranillo |

## 9. Abstract attribute mapping

| Need | Wine attribute | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Cut creamy/buttery sauce | High acidity | Chablis, Champagne, Sauvignon Blanc |
| Match buttery/cream sauce | Concordance — oak/butter | Meursault, Mâcon, Viognier |
| Balance citric herbs | Citric mineral white | Sancerre, Albariño, Verdicchio |
| Balance spice/heat | Off-dry, low alcohol | Riesling Spätlese, Gewürztraminer |
| Match earthy/mushroom | Earthy red | Pinot Noir, Nebbiolo |
| Add structure to rich roast | Body + oak | Oaked Chardonnay, white Burgundy, Viognier |
| Lift fatty pork belly | Acid + slight sweetness | Off-dry Riesling, Gewürztraminer |
| Match sweet glaze | Slight residual sugar | Off-dry Riesling, demi-sec Vouvray |
| Stand up to spicy umami (Asian) | Pinot Noir or aromatic off-dry | Burgundy, Alsace Pinot Gris |

## 10. Service order in a poultry/pork-led meal

- Start: dry sparkling (Champagne Brut Nature, Cava) with pâté or charcuterie
- Light course: Pinot Blanc or Sauvignon Blanc with vegetables/light fish
- Main: oaked Chardonnay (chicken/veal) or Pinot Noir (pork/duck)
- Cheese: light reds or Sauternes
- Dessert: see wine-pairing-desserts

---

## 11. Common mistakes

- **Tannic red with delicate poultry**: tannins overwhelm. Stay light.
- **Oaked Chardonnay with citrus chicken**: the oak clashes with the lemon. Use Sauvignon or Sancerre instead.
- **Dry sparkling with sweet ham glaze**: brut tastes sharp against sugar. Use Demi-sec.
- **Cab Sauvignon with spicy curry**: heat amplified. Pivot to Riesling.
- **High-alcohol wine with delicate veal**: alcohol burns through the meat. Stay below 13.5 %.

## 12. Quick decision tree

```
Is it white meat?
├── Roast chicken (simple, butter) → Chardonnay Bourgogne OR Pinot Noir
├── Fried chicken → Champagne
├── Chicken curry → Off-dry Riesling, Gewürz
├── Coq au vin → Burgundy Pinot Noir
├── Pork loin → Pinot Blanc, Riesling, Beaujolais
├── Pork belly (fatty) → Off-dry Riesling (contrast)
├── Schweinshaxe → Spätburgunder, Riesling Trocken
├── Schnitzel/tonkatsu → Grüner Veltliner
├── Veal cream sauce → Meursault, Viognier
├── Vitello tonnato → Soave, Gavi
├── Osso buco → Barolo, Nebbiolo
├── Rabbit + mushrooms → Bordeaux Supérieur, Pinot Noir
└── Turkey → Pinot Noir, Beaujolais Cru
```
