# Wine Pairing — Cheese

The cheese-and-wine cliché is "red wine, always" — and it's mostly wrong. **White wine usually pairs better with cheese**, except for the hardest aged reds-friendly cheeses. Sweet wines are the secret weapon for blue and strong cheeses.

Read `wine-pairing-fundamentals` first.

---

## 1. The general framework for cheese

Cheese flavour scales with:
- **Salt** (often high)
- **Fat** (often high)
- **Funk / ammonia / umami** (more in aged, washed-rind, blue)
- **Acidity** (high in fresh / goat)

Wine direction:
- **Acidity** in the wine to cut cheese fat → whites usually win
- **Sweetness** to balance salt and funk → dessert wines win for blue
- **Tannin** clashes with most cheese (the rind, fat, and salt confuse tannin)
- **Sparkling** is a brilliant universal partner

Old French principle: ***le fromage demande des contraires*** — cheese asks for contrast (salt vs sweet, fat vs acid).

## 2. Fresh cheese (no rind, eat young)

Mozzarella, burrata, ricotta, mascarpone, fromage blanc, queso fresco, feta, halloumi.

- Profile: high acidity in cheese, low fat (relatively), milky/grassy notes.
- Wine direction: **fresh, aromatic, acidic white** or **light Rosé**.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Sauvignon Blanc** (canonical with caprese / ensalada Caprese)
  - **Pinot Grigio** (Friuli, Alto Adige)
  - **Vermentino** (Liguria)
  - **Albariño**
  - **Trebbiano d'Abruzzo**
  - **Provence rosé**
  - **Vinho Verde**
  - **Cava** / **Prosecco** / **Crémant**
- Feta + Greek salad: **Assyrtiko** (Santorini canonical).
- Mozzarella di bufala: **Falanghina**, **Greco di Tufo**.

---

## 3. Soft-ripened bloomy rind

Brie, Camembert, Brillat-Savarin, Saint-Marcellin, Coulommiers, triple-creams.

- Profile: creamy, fungal/mushroom rind, increasing intensity with age.
- Wine direction: **dry to off-dry white** with body. Light red works for younger Brie.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Champagne / Crémant** (canonical with triple-cream, e.g. Brillat-Savarin)
  - **Chardonnay** (oaked) — Mâconnais, Chablis, Australian
  - **Vouvray demi-sec**
  - **Gewürztraminer** (especially with strong-ripened Brie)
  - **Sauvignon Blanc** (for very young, lemony Brie)
  - **Pinot Noir** (light Burgundy works with Camembert "rôti" — baked)
  - **Cider** for traditional Camembert pairing (Normandy)
- Brie truffé / Brillat-Savarin: **Champagne** (Blanc de Blancs).

---

## 4. Washed-rind / strong-smell cheese

Munster, Maroilles, Époisses, Pont l'Évêque, Livarot, Limburger, Taleggio, Reblochon.

- Profile: pungent ammonia/funk rind, creamy interior, intense umami.
- Wine direction: **aromatic, off-dry white** that mirrors the funk and tames it.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Gewürztraminer** (Alsace canonical for Munster — rosé-coloured, lychee, spice)
  - **Pinot Gris Alsace Vendange Tardive**
  - **Riesling Spätlese**
  - **Marc / eau-de-vie** (traditional accompaniment)
- Époisses: **Marc de Bourgogne** (traditional — the cheese is washed in it), **Mâcon-Villages**, or — risky but works — **Burgundy red**.
- Pont l'Évêque: **Vouvray demi-sec**, **Pommeau de Normandie** (Calvados-based aperitif).
- Reblochon (tartiflette): **Apremont / Roussette de Savoie** (Savoie white).

---

## 5. Semi-soft / semi-hard

Tomme, Saint-Nectaire, Cantal, Edam, Gouda, Fontina, Asiago, Provolone.

- Profile: balanced, nutty, mild to medium intensity.
- Wine direction: **medium-bodied red** or **dry rounded white**.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Côtes du Rhône**, **Beaujolais Cru**, **Saint-Pourçain**, **Sangiovese**
  - **Mâcon-Villages**, **Pouilly-Fuissé**, **Soave Classico**
  - **Fontina** (Aosta): **Carema**, **Donnaz** (Nebbiolo from Aosta/Piedmont border)

---

## 6. Hard aged cheese

Parmigiano-Reggiano, Grana Padano, Pecorino Romano/Toscano, Manchego, Comté, Beaufort, Gruyère, Emmental, aged Cheddar, aged Gouda.

- Profile: crystalline, umami, salty, intense, often crystals (tyrosine).
- Wine direction: **structured red** or **sweet/fortified white**.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Parmigiano-Reggiano**: **Lambrusco** (sparkling red — local), **Barolo**, **Brunello**, aged **Champagne**
  - **Grana Padano**: **Franciacorta**, **Lugana**, **Lambrusco**
  - **Pecorino Romano** (very salty): **Cesanese del Piglio**, **Cannonau di Sardegna**, **Lambrusco**
  - **Pecorino di Pienza** (with mostarde — italysfinestwines): **Vino Nobile di Montepulciano**
  - **Manchego** (canonical with Tempranillo Reserva — kommen / bodeboca):
    - Young Manchego: **Rioja Crianza**
    - Aged Manchego: **Rioja Gran Reserva**, **Ribera del Duero Reserva**
  - **Comté** (Jura):
    - Young (12 mo): **Chardonnay du Jura**, **Vin Jaune** young
    - Aged (24-36 mo): **Vin Jaune** (canonical), **Château-Chalon**, **Savagnin**
    - With nuts/honey: **Vin de Paille** (Jura sweet)
  - **Beaufort / Gruyère** (Savoie / Switzerland): **Vin de Savoie blanc**, **Chasselas** (Swiss), **Jura whites**
  - **Cheddar (aged)**: **Cabernet Sauvignon**, **Zinfandel**, **Port** (vintage)

---

## 7. Goat cheese (chèvre)

Crottin de Chavignol, Sainte-Maure, Sancerre chèvre, Selles-sur-Cher, Valençay, Pélardon.

- Profile: tangy, lactic, slightly chalky to aged-dry.
- Wine direction: **Sauvignon Blanc** is canonical.
- **Concrete (fresh chèvre)**:
  - **Sancerre** / **Pouilly-Fumé** (Loire canonical — same terroir)
  - **New Zealand Sauvignon Blanc**
  - **Touraine SB**
  - **dry Vouvray** (Chenin)
- **Concrete (aged/dry chèvre)**:
  - **demi-sec Vouvray**, **Quincy**, **Reuilly**
  - **rich SB**, **white Burgundy** (Saint-Aubin)
- Warm chèvre on toast (chèvre chaud salad): **Sancerre**, **Pouilly-Fumé**.

---

## 8. Sheep cheese (brebis, ovino)

Roquefort (blue — see §9), Ossau-Iraty, Brebis des Pyrénées, Pecorino, Manchego, Idiazabal.

- Profile: rich, grassy, often with herb/lanolin notes, aged variants nutty.
- Wine direction: **white** (off-dry or aromatic) or **medium red**.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Ossau-Iraty** (Pays Basque): **Jurançon sec** or **Jurançon moelleux** (with black cherry jam — traditional)
  - **Pecorino Toscano**: **Chianti Classico**, **Vino Nobile**, **Vermentino**
  - **Idiazabal** (smoked, Basque): **Txakoli**, **Rioja Crianza**
  - **Manchego**: see §6

---

## 9. Blue cheese

Roquefort, Stilton, Gorgonzola (dolce + piccante), Bleu d'Auvergne, Fourme d'Ambert, Cabrales, Cashel Blue, Danablu.

- Profile: salty, pungent, mould-piquant, fat-rich.
- Wine direction: **sweet wine** is the universal rescue. The salt-sweet contrast is one of the great pairings.
- **Concrete (canonical)**:
  - **Roquefort + Sauternes** (canonical French pairing)
  - **Stilton + Vintage Port** (canonical British pairing)
  - **Gorgonzola piccante + vino passito** (Italian — enotecaravazzani)
  - **Cabrales + Pedro Ximénez** (Spanish — Andalucian sweet sherry)
- **Concrete (alternatives)**:
  - **Sauternes**, **Coteaux du Layon**, **Quart de Chaume**, **Tokaji Aszú**, **Eiswein**, **Banyuls**, **Maury**
  - **Port** — Tawny, LBV, Vintage
- **Fourme d'Ambert / Bleu d'Auvergne**: Sauternes, Jurançon moelleux, vin doux naturel.
- **Gorgonzola dolce** (creamy, less piquant): **Moscato d'Asti** or off-dry Riesling.

---

## 10. Smoked cheese / processed

- Profile: smoke, often firm rubbery texture.
- Wine direction: **smoky red** (concordance) or **off-dry white**.
- **Concrete**: **Rioja**, **Syrah**, **Pinot Noir**, **Riesling Spätlese**.

---

## 11. Cheese boards (mixed plateau de fromages)

You can't satisfy every cheese with one wine, but some are versatile.

- **Sauternes / late-harvest** — covers blue + soft cheeses
- **Champagne Brut** — covers most except very strong washed-rind
- **Vin Jaune / Sherry Amontillado / Madeira Sercial** — covers hard aged + nuts + dried fruit
- **Off-dry Vouvray demi-sec** — covers most
- **Lambrusco** — fun with charcuterie/cheese boards (Emilian table tradition)
- **White Burgundy (Meursault)** — covers Brie + hard
- **Vino passito (Italian)** — covers blue + aged hard (especially Italian boards)
- **Tawny Port** — covers blue + cheddar-style

A trick from chamvermeil: **a single sweet white covers a mixed board with fresh + aged**. The sweetness handles both.

## 12. Regional cheese-and-wine ("what grows together")

- **Bordeaux**: Bordeaux Supérieur blanc with cheese plates; Sauternes + Roquefort
- **Burgundy**: Pinot Noir with Époisses, white Burgundy with Comté and Brie de Meaux
- **Champagne**: Champagne with Chaource and Langres
- **Loire**: Sancerre with Crottin de Chavignol
- **Alsace**: Gewürztraminer with Munster
- **Savoie/Jura**: Apremont with Reblochon, Vin Jaune with Comté
- **Tuscany**: Chianti with Pecorino Toscano
- **Piedmont**: Barolo with Castelmagno
- **Emilia-Romagna**: Lambrusco with Parmigiano-Reggiano
- **La Rioja**: Tempranillo with Manchego
- **Andalucía**: Pedro Ximénez with Cabrales / blue

## 14. Abstract attribute mapping

| Cheese trait | Wine attribute | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| High salt | Sweetness (contrast) | Sauternes, Port, Tokaji, PX |
| High fat / creaminess | Acidity | Sancerre, Champagne, dry Riesling |
| Ammonia / funky rind | Aromatic off-dry | Gewürztraminer, Pinot Gris VT, Vouvray demi-sec |
| Umami / aged-crystalline | Structured red OR sweet | Barolo, Lambrusco, Vin Jaune, vintage Port |
| Sour / lactic (fresh) | High acid, aromatic | Sauvignon Blanc, Vermentino, Cava |
| Smoky | Smoky red | Rioja, Syrah |
| Crunchy crystals (Parmigiano, aged Gouda) | Sparkling red OR structured red | Lambrusco, Barolo, vintage Port |

## 15. Common myths and mistakes

❌ **"Red wine with cheese."** Often wrong. Most cheese pairs better with white. Tannin clashes with salt, fat, and funky rinds.

❌ **"Brut Champagne with sweet blue cheese."** Sweetness mismatch — brut tastes harsh. Use Sauternes.

❌ **"Same red through the whole cheese board."** Use a sweet wine to cover the whole board.

❌ **"Cabernet with goat."** The goat acidity neutralises the tannin, leaving the cheese flat. Use Sauvignon Blanc.

❌ **"Big oaked Chardonnay with washed-rind."** Oak collides with ammonia. Use Gewürz or off-dry Pinot Gris.

❌ **"Vintage Port with everything."** Save Port for Stilton, cheddar, and chocolate desserts — not Brie or fresh.

## 16. Quick decision tree

```
What family is the cheese?
├── Fresh (mozzarella, feta, ricotta) → Sauvignon Blanc / Vermentino / Cava
├── Soft bloomy (Brie, Camembert) → Champagne / Chardonnay / Vouvray demi-sec
├── Washed-rind (Munster, Époisses) → Gewürztraminer / Pinot Gris VT
├── Semi-hard (Tomme, Fontina) → Côtes du Rhône / Mâcon
├── Hard aged (Parmigiano, Manchego, Comté) → Barolo / Lambrusco / Rioja GR / Vin Jaune
├── Goat (Crottin, Sainte-Maure) → Sancerre / Pouilly-Fumé
├── Sheep (Ossau-Iraty, Pecorino) → Jurançon / Chianti / Manchego logic
├── Blue (Roquefort, Stilton, Gorgonzola) → Sauternes / Port / Vino Passito
├── Smoked → Rioja / Syrah / Riesling Spätlese
└── Mixed board → Sauternes OR Champagne OR Vouvray demi-sec
```

## 17. Service order at a cheese course

If serving the cheese course with one wine, choose:
- Last red of the meal (if it's structured)
- A sweet wine if blue cheese is included
- Always serve cheese at room temperature; serve whites slightly less cold than usual to release aroma
