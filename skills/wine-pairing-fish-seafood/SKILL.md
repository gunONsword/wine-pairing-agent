# Wine Pairing — Fish & Seafood

The most acid-sensitive category. Wines need **freshness + salinity + minerality** for most preparations, with notable exceptions for oily fish and rich sauces.

Read `wine-pairing-fundamentals` first.

---

## 1. The general framework

Fish has:
- **Delicate, sweet-tendency flesh** that high tannin masks with a metallic / fishy aftertaste.
- **Iodine / salinity** from the sea.
- Often a **citrus or vinegar squeeze** that lifts the dish.
- **Lipid profile**: lean (white fish) vs. fatty (salmon, tuna, mackerel, swordfish).

Default wine direction:
- **White, mineral, dry** for lean/raw/poached fish
- **White, rounder, oaked** for fish with cream or butter sauces
- **Light red, low tannin, fresh acidity** for fatty fish or in red-sauce preparations
- **Champagne / sparkling** for oysters, fried fish, and as the universal aperitif

Avoid: high tannin reds with any seafood except specific cases (tuna alla pizzaiola, salmon with cherry sauce, swordfish in red sauce).

## 2. Lean / white fish

### 2.1 Cod (cabillaud, morue, bacalao)

- Profile: clean, slightly sweet, flaky.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Sauvignon Blanc** — Loire (Sancerre, Pouilly-Fumé, Touraine), New Zealand (Marlborough), Chile
  - **Albariño** — Rías Baixas (Galicia) — the canonical Iberian match
  - **Verdejo** — Rueda
  - **Soave Classico** — Veneto
  - **Picpoul de Pinet** — Languedoc
- With cream sauce: **Chablis**, **Mâconnais white**.

### 2.2 Sea bass (bar, loup de mer, branzino, lubina)

- Delicate, tender, mineral-friendly.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Sauvignon Blanc** (canonical — Sancerre, Pouilly-Fumé)
  - **Albariño**
  - **Vermentino** — Liguria, Sardinia
  - **Verdicchio dei Castelli di Jesi** — Marche
  - **Chablis**
  - **dry Riesling** (Alsace, Mosel trocken)
- Grilled with herbs: **Vermentino**, **Picpoul**.
- Whole fish in salt crust: **Champagne Blanc de Blancs**.

### 2.3 Sole / turbot / John Dory (sole, turbot, saint-pierre)

- Delicate, expensive, often with refined sauces.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Meursault**, **Puligny-Montrachet** (white Burgundy — canonical for sauce dishes)
  - **Chablis Premier Cru**
  - **Champagne** (Blanc de Blancs)
- Sole meunière (butter, lemon): rich Chardonnay or Champagne.
- Turbot in sauce: oaked Chardonnay.

### 2.4 Sea bream (dorade, dorada, orata)

- Mediterranean classic.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Vermentino**, **Albariño**, **Greco di Tufo**, **Falanghina**, **Fiano di Avellino**
  - **Provence rosé** (especially with grilled bream)
  - **Sherry Fino / Manzanilla** (especially for grilled or with anchovies)
  - **Amontillado** (with Andalucian dorada with Amontillado sauce — sherry.wine)

### 2.5 Hake (merluza, merlu)

- Lean, mild, popular in Spanish cuisine.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Albariño**, **Godello** (Galicia), **Txakoli** (Basque Country), **Verdejo** (Rueda)
  - **Chablis**, **Muscadet** (with green sauce — merluza en salsa verde)

### 2.6 Monkfish (lotte, rape)

- Firm, almost meaty texture.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Oaked Chardonnay** (white Burgundy, Australian)
  - **Vermentino**, **Verdicchio**
  - **light Pinot Noir** (with red sauce or American style preparation)

### 2.7 Sea bass / branzino with risotto / pasta

- See wine-pairing-pasta-pizza.

### 2.8 Red snapper (vivaneau, pargo)

- **Concrete**: **White Rhône blend** (Roussanne-Marsanne), **Vermentino**, **Verdicchio**, **Soave**.

### 2.9 Cabillaud en papillote / steamed white fish

- **Mâconnais white**, **Chablis**, **Picpoul**, **Soave**.

### 2.10 Fish soup (sopa de pescado, soupe de poisson, brodetto, cacciucco)

- **Concrete**:
  - **Manzanilla de Sanlúcar** (canonical with Spanish fish soup)
  - **Macabeo, Viura, Verdejo** (Spanish dry whites)
  - **Provence rosé** (Bandol rosé canonical with bouillabaisse)
  - **Cassis blanc** (Marsanne-Clairette)

### 2.11 Bouillabaisse (Marseille)

- **Bandol rosé** (canonical Provençal)
- **Cassis blanc**
- **Spanish rosé**

### 2.12 Fritto misto / fried fish / fish & chips

- Acid + bubbles cut grease.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Champagne / Cava / Crémant** (canonical)
  - **English sparkling**
  - **Trebbiano d'Abruzzo**
  - **Pinot Grigio**
  - **Verdicchio**
  - **Manzanilla / Fino Sherry** (for calamares fritos)

## 3. Oily / fatty fish

These have more flavour, more fat, and can take more wine — including light reds.

### 3.1 Salmon

- The most versatile oily fish. Treatment determines the wine:
- **Raw / cured (gravlax, sashimi)**: **dry Riesling**, **Champagne Blanc de Blancs**, **Albariño**, **Sancerre**.
- **Smoked salmon**: **Champagne Blanc de Blancs** (Brut), **Sancerre**, **dry Riesling**, **Fino Sherry**.
- **Grilled or baked salmon**: **Pinot Noir** (Burgundy, Oregon, Russian River Valley) — canonical for cooked salmon. Oily fish + light tannin red works because tannins are gentle and fish fat tames them.
- **Salmon with dill / cream**: **Pinot Noir** or **oaked Chardonnay**.
- **Salmon with olive butter and orzo**: **Russian River Valley Pinot Noir**.
- **Salmon tarator (Mediterranean / Turkish, tahini, lemon)**: **Riesling** (Margaret River, Mosel).

### 3.2 Tuna (thon)

- **Concrete**:
  - **Raw (tartare, sashimi)**: **Champagne**, **Albariño**, **dry Riesling**
  - **Seared rare**: **Pinot Noir**, **Rosé** (Tavel, Bandol)
  - **In tomato sauce (alla pizzaiola, à la sicilienne)**: **light Italian red** — Frappato, light Nero d'Avola, Cerasuolo di Vittoria, Sangiovese
  - **Vitello tonnato** (cold): **Soave**, **Gavi**, **Franciacorta**
  - **Tuna and egg baguette / niçoise**: **Tavel rosé**, **Provence rosé**

### 3.3 Swordfish (espadon, pesce spada)

- Firm, meaty, often grilled.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Grilled / plain**: **Vermentino**, **Verdicchio**, **rosé**, **light Sangiovese**
  - **Sicilian style (with capers, olives, tomato — alla pizzaiola)**: **Frappato**, **Nero d'Avola** (light), or even a soft Sangiovese
  - **Rouget (red mullet)**: **light red** can work — Cabernet Franc Chinon, Bourgueil

### 3.4 Sardine, anchovy, mackerel (sarde, anchois, maquereau, sgombro)

- Strong, oily, often vinegared.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Vinho Verde** (Portugal — canonical with grilled sardines)
  - **Manzanilla / Fino** (with anchovies in vinegar)
  - **Albariño**, **dry Riesling**
  - **Light Sangiovese** for passatelli al sgombro (mackerel pasta + Sangiovese, Caiarossa style — perlagesuite)

### 3.5 Red mullet (rouget, triglia)

- **Concrete**: **Provence rosé**, **light Pinot Noir**, **Cabernet Franc** (Chinon, Bourgueil), **Cassis blanc**, **Marsanne** (e.g. Cuvée Le Cadet, Domaine Grand-Père Jules — rouget with tapenade).

### 3.6 Grilled mackerel

- **Concrete**: **Vinho Verde**, **Albariño**, **Sauvignon Blanc**.

## 4. Shellfish & crustaceans

### 4.1 Oysters (huîtres, ostriche)

- High salinity, mineral, iodine.
- **Concrete (canonical)**:
  - **Champagne Blanc de Blancs** or **Brut Nature**
  - **Chablis** (Premier Cru ideal)
  - **Muscadet Sèvre-et-Maine sur lie** (Loire — historical pairing)
  - **Sancerre**
  - **Albariño**
  - **Picpoul de Pinet**
  - **Manzanilla / Fino**

### 4.2 Mussels (moules, cozze, mejillones)

- **Concrete**:
  - **Muscadet** (canonical with moules marinières)
  - **Sauvignon Blanc** (Loire)
  - **Bordeaux blanc** (Sauvignon-Sémillon — see Château La Rame from planete-bordeaux risotto aux moules)
  - **Albariño**
  - **Vinho Verde**
- Mussels in white wine (marinière): the same SB or Muscadet used to cook.
- Mussels Provençal: **Chilean Sauvignon Blanc** or Provence rosé.

### 4.3 Clams (palourdes, vongole, almejas)

- **Concrete**:
  - **Pinot Grigio** (light, fresh — canonical with spaghetti alle vongole — see pasta skill)
  - **Verdicchio**, **Soave**
  - **Albariño**
  - **Greco di Tufo** (for spaghetti with cockles)

### 4.4 Scallops (coquilles Saint-Jacques, capesante, vieiras)

- Sweet, delicate, often pan-seared.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Chablis** (canonical Saint-Jacques)
  - **Albariño** (canonical for ceviche de vieira)
  - **Meursault** (with cream sauce)
  - **Champagne**
  - **Sancerre**
  - **Vouvray sec**

### 4.5 Prawns / shrimp (gambas, langoustines)

- **Concrete**:
  - **Manzanilla / Fino** (canonical with gamba blanca de Huelva)
  - **Albariño**
  - **Cava / Champagne**
  - **Vermentino**
- Spicy grilled shrimp (Mexican / Asian): **Mencía** (Bierzo, for spicy shrimp stew per Wine Enthusiast), **Riesling Spätlese**.
- Bisque de gambas: **Champagne Jacquesson** style — perlagesuite.

### 4.6 Langoustines / scampi

- **Concrete**: **Chablis Premier Cru**, **Meursault**, **Champagne**, **Riesling Alsace**.

### 4.7 Lobster (homard, langosta)

- Rich, sweet, demands premium.
- **Concrete**:
  - **Oaked Chardonnay** (Meursault, Puligny-Montrachet, Napa Chardonnay)
  - **Champagne**
  - **Riesling Spätlese trocken**
- Lobster with butter / thermidor: **oaked Chardonnay**.
- Lobster bisque: **Sauternes** (contrast — rich sweet on rich savoury) or oaked Chardonnay.

### 4.8 Crab (crabe, granchio)

- **Concrete**:
  - **Riesling** (dry — Mosel, Alsace, Australian)
  - **Sauvignon Blanc**
  - **Chablis**
  - **Champagne**
- Crab cake / Crab in chilled corn soup: **Australian Chardonnay**.

### 4.9 Shrimp / lobster with red prawns (Sicilian Mazara prawns)

- **Concrete**: **Vermentino di Gallura**, **Etna Bianco**, **Champagne**, **Verdicchio**.

### 4.10 Mixed shellfish platter / plateau de fruits de mer

- **Concrete**: **Muscadet sur lie**, **Sancerre**, **Albariño**, **Cava**, **Champagne**, **Picpoul**.

### 4.11 Spicy / Asian shrimp

- **Off-dry Riesling**, **Gewürztraminer**, **Cava demi-sec**.

## 5. Squid, octopus, cuttlefish

### 5.1 Fried squid (calamares fritos)

- **Manzanilla** (canonical Spanish)
- **Cava**
- **Verdejo**

### 5.2 Grilled octopus (poulpe, polpo, pulpo)

- **Concrete**:
  - **Albariño** (canonical with Galician pulpo a la gallega)
  - **Godello**
  - **Vermentino**
  - **light Rosé**

### 5.3 Cuttlefish (seiche, sepia)

- **Bordeaux blanc** (e.g. Château Les Clauzots Graves blanc — chamvermeil for seiches à la Ferret Capienne)
- **Albariño**, **Sauvignon Blanc**, **Verdicchio**

### 5.4 Pasta with octopus (e.g. spaghettone with garlic, oil, peperoncino, octopus — Castellare)

- **Le Ginestre** (Chardonnay + Sauvignon Blanc blend, Castellare)
- **Vermentino**, **Greco di Tufo**

## 6. Raw fish & cured preparations

### 6.1 Sashimi / sushi (Japanese)

- **Concrete**:
  - **Champagne** (Blanc de Blancs ideal)
  - **dry Riesling** (especially with wasabi — sweetness balances heat)
  - **Sancerre**
  - **Sauvignon Blanc**
  - **Albariño**
  - **Manzanilla** (a contrarian choice that works — salt + iodine)
  - Avoid: anything oaked, anything tannic.

### 6.2 Ceviche / aguachile (Peruvian/Mexican raw fish in citrus)

- **Concrete**:
  - **Albariño** (canonical with ceviche de vieira — bodeboca)
  - **Sauvignon Blanc**
  - **Verdejo**
  - **Sherry Manzanilla** (for ceviche con Manzanilla — sherry.wine)

### 6.3 Carpaccio of fish (tuna, sea bass, salmon)

- **Champagne**, **dry Riesling**, **Albariño**, **Sancerre**.

### 6.4 Smoked salmon / smoked trout / smoked mackerel

- **Champagne Blanc de Blancs** (Brut, canonical)
- **dry Riesling**
- **Sancerre / Pouilly-Fumé**
- **Vintage Brut Champagne** with caviar + smoked salmon

### 6.5 Caviar

- **Champagne Brut Blanc de Blancs** (canonical)
- **Premium dry Riesling**
- **Sherry Manzanilla** (a confident pick)

### 6.6 Salted cod (bacalao, brandade de morue)

- **Albariño**, **Godello**, **Cava**, **Manzanilla**.

## 7. Sushi / sashimi specifics

| Item | Wine |
|---|---|
| Salmon nigiri | Pinot Noir (cool climate) or dry Riesling |
| Tuna (akami, chu-toro, o-toro) | Champagne; toro can take Pinot Noir |
| White fish (hirame, tai) | Chablis, Sauvignon Blanc |
| Uni (sea urchin) | Champagne or aged Riesling |
| Eel (unagi) — sweet sauce | Off-dry Riesling, Gewürztraminer |
| Ikura (salmon roe) | Champagne |
| Spicy tuna roll | Off-dry Riesling, Cava |
| Wasabi-heavy | Off-dry Riesling, Gewürz |

| Sauce / preparation | Wine direction |

| Beurre blanc | Muscadet, Chablis, Sancerre |
| Hollandaise | Chablis, Champagne |
| Lemon-butter | Sauvignon Blanc, Pouilly-Fumé |

| Saffron / safron (paella) | Albariño, Cava, dry Rosé |

## 9. Abstract attribute mapping

| Dish trait | Wine attribute | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Iodine / brine | Salinity, mineral | Chablis, Muscadet, Manzanilla, Albariño |
| Lemon / citrus squeeze | High acidity, citric profile | Sauvignon Blanc, Sancerre |
| Cream / butter sauce | Body + acid balance | Meursault, Mâconnais |
| Smoke / grilled crust | Mineral + flint | Pouilly-Fumé, Albariño Godello |
| Fatty / oily fish | Either acidity or light red tannin | Riesling, Pinot Noir |
| Soft, sweet flesh | Soft, off-dry | Vouvray sec, dry Riesling |
| Strong / oily (sardine, mackerel) | High acidity, mineral | Vinho Verde, Manzanilla |
| Fried / oily texture | Bubbles + acid | Champagne, Cava |
| Spicy heat (Asian) | Off-dry + low alcohol | Riesling Spätlese, Gewürz |

## 10. Service order in a fish-led meal

- Apéritif: Champagne / Cava
- Starter (oysters, sushi): Chablis or Champagne Blanc de Blancs
- Soup (bouillabaisse, fish soup): Provence rosé or Manzanilla
- Main (grilled / sauced fish): Sancerre / Albariño / Meursault depending on richness
- Cheese: usually skipped on a fish menu; if served go to oaked Chardonnay
- Dessert: Sauternes / Eiswein for citrus desserts

---

## 11. Common mistakes

- **Oaked Chardonnay with sushi**: oak collides with raw fish and soy. Use Champagne or dry Riesling.
- **Cabernet Sauvignon with shellfish**: metallic taste. Switch to white or Pinot.
- **Brut Champagne with sweet teriyaki sushi**: too dry. Switch to off-dry Riesling.
- **High-tannin red with grilled tuna**: avoid; choose Pinot or rosé.
- **Sweet wine with oysters**: kills the brine. Use Brut Nature.
- **Heavy oaky white with sole meunière**: overpowers. Use a balanced Meursault, not a butter-bomb.

## 12. Quick decision tree

```
Is it fish/seafood?
├── Oysters / raw shellfish → Champagne / Chablis / Muscadet
├── Sushi/sashimi → Champagne / dry Riesling
├── Lean white fish (cod, bass, sole) → Sauvignon Blanc / Albariño / Chablis
├── Lean fish + cream → Meursault / Chablis Premier Cru
├── Oily fish (salmon, tuna) → Pinot Noir / dry Riesling
├── Tuna or swordfish + tomato → Light Sicilian red (Frappato, Nero d'Avola)
├── Bouillabaisse / fish soup → Bandol rosé / Manzanilla
├── Fried fish / chips → Champagne / Cava
├── Shellfish (mussels, clams) → Muscadet / Pinot Grigio / Albariño
├── Lobster (rich) → Oaked Chardonnay (Meursault)
├── Spicy/Asian seafood → Off-dry Riesling / Gewürz
└── Smoked fish → Champagne Blanc de Blancs / Riesling
```
